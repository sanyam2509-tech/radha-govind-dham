Jagadguru Shri Kripalu Ji Maharaj

The Supreme Spiritual Master of the World

Jagadguru Shri Kripalu Ji Maharaj was not just a Guru; he was the very embodiment of Divine Love (Premanand). In an era dominated by materialism, he descended to reconcile the conflicting philosophies of the world and reveal the simplest, most accessible path to God Realization for the modern age.

The Divine Appearance

He appeared on the auspicious night of **Sharad Purnima** in October 1922, in the small village of Mangarh, near Allahabad. From his childhood, his divine nature was evident. Although he completed his formal education in Kashi (Varanasi) in merely a few years—mastering Sanskrit grammar, Vedas, and scriptures effortlessly—his true mission was to distribute the bliss of Bhakti.

The Title of Jagadguru

In 1957, at the tender age of 34, he addressed the **Kashi Vidvat Parishat**, the supreme body of 500 scholars in Varanasi. He spoke for ten days in complex Sanskrit, citing thousands of scriptures from memory without referring to a single book.

"We are stunned. He is not merely a scholar; he is the personification of the Vedas. We declare him as Jagadgurottam—the Supreme among all Jagadgurus."
- Kashi Vidvat Parishat, 1957

He became the **5th Original Jagadguru** in history, following the lineage of Adi Shankaracharya, Nimbarkacharya, Ramanujacharya, and Madhvacharya. He was merely 34, while previous Jagadgurus received this title much later in life.

His Philosophy: Roopdhyan

Maharaj Ji simplified the Vedas into one practical instruction: **Attach your mind to God.** He taught that physical actions (rituals) alone do not purify the soul; it is the mind's attachment that matters. He introduced Roopdhyan (meditating on the form of Radha Krishna) combined with Sankirtan (chanting) as the most effective method for this age.

Monumental Literature

To ensure his teachings lasted forever, he authored masterpieces such as:

  • Prem Ras Madira: 1008 devotional songs (pads) describing the pastimes of Radha Krishna.
  • Prem Ras Siddhant: The philosophy of Divine Love explained in simple Hindi.
  • Radha Govind Geet: Couplets of wisdom for daily life.

Prem Mandir, Vrindavan - A monument of his Grace.
